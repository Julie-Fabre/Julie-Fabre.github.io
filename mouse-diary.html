The Mouse's Diary

Found under the floorboards of a suspiciously interactive room

Day 1

Moved in today. The rent is free and there's a loose plank for easy access. The human seems nice, if a bit obsessed with brains.

Day 12

The human spent 6 hours staring at squiggly lines on her monitor. She called them spikes. They looked nothing like spikes. I've seen spikes. The cat next door has spikes. These are just wiggly lines.

Day 23

She brought home shoes today. Handmade. I tried one on while she was sleeping. It was enormous. Left a tiny footprint inside. She'll never know.

Day 34

Something troubling. She has a poster about basal ganglia. I don't know what ganglia are but they sound like a type of cheese. I'm going to investigate tonight.

Day 41

Ganglia are not cheese. They are not cheese at all. I don't want to talk about it.

Day 47

The human clicked the plank again. I played dead. She bought it.

Day 58

The jar creatures are fermenting again. The bubbling keeps me up at night. I've named them: Gerald, Bertha, and The Blob. Gerald is my favourite. He has the most consistent bubble rhythm.

Day 63

She keeps folding pieces of leather into shapes. Today she made a handbag. I could live in that handbag. I am considering it.

Day 72

Disaster. She got a Tintin rocket on the shelf. Spent the whole night inspecting it before I realised it couldn't actually launch. My heart is still racing.

Day 89

The lamp went off. Finally, my time to explore. I found crumbs on the desk. Premium crumbs. The artisan kind. She must be eating something fancy up there.

Day 103

She turned the lamp back on. I wasn't ready. Froze mid-crumb. We made eye contact. Neither of us has mentioned it since.

Day 115

Someone keeps looking at me through a glowing rectangle. They move a little arrow around and sometimes it hovers RIGHT OVER MY HIDING SPOT. I don't like it. I don't like it at all.

Day 127

The plant on the shelf got bigger today. Nobody watered it. I am suspicious. I have drawn a diagram of possible plant-related conspiracies on the back of this page.

Day 134

The drawer opened by itself. Inside: an old paper. I read it. I understood none of it. There were graphs. One of them had error bars. I don't know what error bars are but they seem important to humans.

Day 152

She closed the curtains today. It got very dark. Then she opened them again. Then closed them. Then opened. I think this is some kind of experiment. I am the control group.

Day 168

Good news: found the coffee mug unattended. Bad news: it was empty. Someone drank it all. Suspects: the human (primary), Gerald (unlikely but not impossible).

Day 181

I have been here for half a year. The floorboard is still loose. The human still hasn't fixed it. I am beginning to think she leaves it loose on purpose. For me. I choose to believe this.

Day 195

There's a clock on the wall now. It ticks. All day. All night. Tick tick tick. I have filed a formal complaint (chewed a small corner of the baseboard).

Day 210

If you are reading this, you found my diary. Please do not tell the human. She thinks this is her office. It is also my office. We are colleagues. She just doesn't know it yet.
