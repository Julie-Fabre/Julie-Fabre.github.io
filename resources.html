Corticostriatal communication

The cortex and striatum are massively interconnected, but how activity in one maps onto the other was unclear. Using large-scale Neuropixels recordings across both structures in mice, we found that striatal activity topographically mirrors cortical activity: nearby cortical regions project to nearby striatal regions, and the tuning of striatal neurons closely reflects that of their cortical inputs.

This means the striatum doesn't just receive cortical signals — it preserves their spatial organisation, carrying a detailed map of cortical representations.

Striatal activity topographically reflects cortical activity
