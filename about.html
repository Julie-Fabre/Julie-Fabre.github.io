About me

I like taking things apart to figure out how they work — brains, mostly, but also fictional rockets and the odd pair of shoes.

I'm a systems neuroscientist and Schmidt Science Fellow in Ilana Witten's lab at Princeton. My PhD was with Kenneth Harris and Matteo Carandini at UCL, where I studied how sensory and motor signals transform across the basal ganglia using large-scale Neuropixels recordings. Before that I studied neuroscience at the École Normale Supérieure in Paris, and medicine at Paris Descartes.

I care about open science and build tools to make electrophysiology data easier to work with: BombCell for spike sorting quality control and UnitMatch for tracking neurons across recording sessions (Nature Methods).

When I'm not in the lab, I make shoes and leather goods by hand — I taught myself from a 19th-century cordwainer's manual after my favourite pair broke and turned out to be unrepairable. I showed some of my work at London Craft Week 2024. I also ferment things and build the occasional pair of LED cycling gloves.
