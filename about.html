About me

I like figuring out how things work — brains, mostly, but also fictional rockets and the odd pair of shoes.

I'm a systems neuroscientist and Schmidt Science Fellow in Ilana Witten's lab at Princeton, where I'm studying how the brain interacts with the peripheral immune system. My PhD was with Kenneth Harris and Matteo Carandini at UCL, where I used large-scale Neuropixels recordings to study how the basal ganglia transform sensory information into motor commands. Before that I studied medicine at Paris Descartes and Paris Diderot, and neuroscience at the École Normale Supérieure, as part of the INSERM MD-PhD program.

I care about open science and build tools to make electrophysiology data easier to work with. One example is BombCell, a tool for automated spike sorting quality control.

When I'm not in the lab, I make shoes and leather goods by hand — I taught myself from a 19th-century cordwainer's manual after my favourite pair broke and turned out to be unrepairable. I showed some of my work at London Craft Week 2024. I also ferment things and build the occasional pair of LED cycling gloves.
