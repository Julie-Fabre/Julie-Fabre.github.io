About me

I am a systems neuroscientist in Ilana Witten's lab at Princeton University. I study how neural circuits across the brain coordinate to give rise to behaviour, using large-scale electrophysiology (Neuropixels) in mice.

I did my PhD with Kenneth Harris and Matteo Carandini at UCL, where I worked on corticostriatal communication and developed open-source tools for spike sorting quality control (BombCell) and cross-session neuron tracking (UnitMatch). Before that, I studied neuroscience at the ENS (Paris) and medicine before that.

Outside of the lab, I make shoes and leather goods by hand, and occasionally investigate whether fictional rockets could actually fly.
